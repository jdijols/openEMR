# JNC 8 Evidence-Based Guideline for the Management of High Blood Pressure in Adults (2014)

Source URL: https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/1791497

## Treatment Initiation Threshold — General Population

In the general population aged 60 years or older, pharmacologic treatment to lower blood pressure should be initiated at systolic blood pressure (SBP) of 150 mm Hg or greater or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) of 90 mm Hg or greater, with a treatment goal of SBP less than 150 mm Hg and DBP less than 90 mm Hg. This is a strong recommendation (Grade A).

## Treatment Threshold Younger Than 60

In the general population younger than 60 years, pharmacologic treatment should be initiated at DBP of 90 mm Hg or greater with a goal DBP of less than 90 mm Hg (Grade A for ages 30-59) and at SBP of 140 mm Hg or greater with a goal SBP of less than 140 mm Hg (Expert Opinion - Grade E).

## Diabetes Mellitus

In the population aged 18 years or older with diabetes, pharmacologic treatment should be initiated at SBP of 140 mm Hg or greater or DBP of 90 mm Hg or greater, with a treatment goal of SBP less than 140 mm Hg and DBP less than 90 mm Hg (Expert Opinion - Grade E). Treatment recommendations apply regardless of comorbid CVD risk factors.

## Chronic Kidney Disease

In the population aged 18 years or older with chronic kidney disease, pharmacologic treatment should be initiated at SBP of 140 mm Hg or greater or DBP of 90 mm Hg or greater with a goal SBP less than 140 mm Hg and DBP less than 90 mm Hg (Expert Opinion - Grade E). Initial therapy should include an ACE inhibitor or ARB to improve kidney outcomes.

## First-Line Therapy — Non-Black Population

In the general non-Black population, including those with diabetes, initial antihypertensive treatment should include a thiazide-type diuretic, calcium channel blocker (CCB), angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor (ACEI), or angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB). Lisinopril, the typical ACEI choice, has demonstrated mortality reduction in multiple randomized trials. (Moderate Recommendation - Grade B).

## First-Line Therapy — Black Population

In the general Black population, including those with diabetes, initial antihypertensive treatment should include a thiazide-type diuretic or CCB. ACEI or ARB monotherapy is less effective in this population (Moderate Recommendation - Grade B for general Black; Grade C for Black with diabetes).

## Stepwise Intensification

If goal blood pressure is not reached within a month of treatment, increase the dose of the initial drug or add a second drug from one of the four recommended classes (thiazide-type diuretic, CCB, ACEI, ARB). Continue to assess BP and adjust the regimen until goal is reached. Three-drug therapy combining a thiazide-type diuretic, CCB, and ACEI/ARB is appropriate when needed; ACEI and ARB should not be used together in the same patient (strong recommendation against the combination).

## Treatment Resistance

If the goal blood pressure cannot be reached using only the drugs in the recommendation, drugs from other classes can be added. Referral to a clinician with expertise in the management of hypertension may be indicated for patients in whom the goal cannot be attained using the above strategy or for the management of complicated patients for whom additional clinical consultation is needed.

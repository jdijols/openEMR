# ADA Standards of Care in Diabetes — Glycemic Targets (2024)

Source URL: https://diabetesjournals.org/care/issue/47/Supplement_1

## Hemoglobin A1C Goal — General

A reasonable A1C goal for many nonpregnant adults with diabetes is less than 7%, achieved with consideration of patient circumstances, risk for hypoglycemia, life expectancy, and presence of advanced microvascular or macrovascular complications. Less stringent A1C goals (such as less than 8%) may be appropriate for patients with a history of severe hypoglycemia, limited life expectancy, advanced complications, or extensive comorbid conditions.

## Comprehensive Cardiovascular Risk Reduction

Comprehensive cardiovascular risk reduction is essential for adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus. This includes blood pressure control to less than 130/80 mm Hg in most adults with diabetes, statin therapy per guideline indications, antiplatelet therapy in select patients, and smoking cessation.

## Statin Therapy in Diabetes

For adults with diabetes ages 40-75 years, the ADA recommends moderate-intensity statin therapy in addition to lifestyle therapy. For adults with diabetes ages 40-75 years at higher cardiovascular risk, including those with one or more atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk factors, the ADA recommends high-intensity statin therapy to reduce LDL-C by 50% or more from baseline and to a target LDL-C of less than 70 mg/dL. Persistent LDL-C elevation above 70 mg/dL on maximum-tolerated statin warrants consideration of ezetimibe or PCSK9 inhibitor add-on.

## Lipid Targets

In adults with diabetes who have established ASCVD or 10-year ASCVD risk of 20% or higher, addition of ezetimibe or a PCSK9 inhibitor to maximum-tolerated statin therapy is reasonable to achieve LDL-C reduction of 50% or more and an LDL-C goal of less than 55 mg/dL. Triglycerides 175 mg/dL or higher on statin therapy may warrant evaluation for secondary causes and consideration of icosapent ethyl in those with established ASCVD or other risk factors.

## First-Line Pharmacotherapy

In most adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus who require pharmacologic therapy, metformin is the preferred initial pharmacologic agent. Metformin should be continued as long as it is tolerated and not contraindicated; other agents, including insulin, should be added to metformin. Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) and sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitors (SGLT2i) with proven cardiovascular benefit are recommended in patients with established ASCVD or high cardiovascular risk independent of A1C.

## SGLT2 Inhibitors and GLP-1 Agonists

For patients with type 2 diabetes and established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, multiple cardiovascular risk factors, or diabetic kidney disease, an SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 receptor agonist with demonstrated cardiovascular disease benefit is recommended as part of the comprehensive cardiovascular risk reduction or glucose-lowering regimens. These recommendations are independent of baseline A1C, individualized A1C target, or metformin use.

## Self-Monitoring of Blood Glucose

Most patients on intensive insulin regimens (multiple-dose insulin or insulin pump therapy) should perform self-monitoring of blood glucose (or use continuous glucose monitoring) prior to meals and snacks, at bedtime, occasionally postprandially, prior to exercise, when low blood glucose is suspected, after treating low blood glucose until normoglycemic, and prior to and while performing critical tasks such as driving.

## Hypoglycemia Awareness and Prevention

In persons with type 2 diabetes treated with insulin, structured education and dose adjustment, including continuous glucose monitoring when appropriate, can substantially reduce hypoglycemia. Patients should be instructed to carry a fast-acting glucose source (15-20 g) and to recognize symptoms; clinicians should screen for hypoglycemia unawareness at every visit in patients on insulin or sulfonylureas.
